‚ùì The Challenge

  • Data Scarcity: "Normal" X-rays are abundant. "Disease" data is rare, expensive, and heavily regulated (HIPAA).
  • The "Black Swan" Failure: Standard AI classifiers only find diseases they were trained on. They miss novel or rare pathologies.
  • Lack of Explainability: Doctors don't trust "Black Box" scores. They need to know where the problem is.
üõë

üí° The Solution: One-Class Learning

Instead of training on diseases, we train only on Healthy Anatomy.

The Logic:

  1. Model learns the "Manifold of Health".
  2. It can reconstruction healthy lungs perfectly.
  3. When it sees a disease (Anomaly), it fails to reconstruct it.
  4. Input - Output = Difference Map (The Lesion)

üß† Three Architectures Compared

Model Mechanism Strengths Weaknesses
VAE Probabilistic Compression (Gaussian Latent Space) Smooth, stable training. Excellent baseline. Blurry reconstructions.
GAN Adversarial Game (Generator vs Discriminator) Sharp, realistic details. High contrast maps. Unstable training (Mode Collapse).
ViT Self-Attention (16x16 Patches) Understands global context & structure. Data hungry; blocky artifacts on small datasets.
