‚ùì The Challenge

  • Data Scarcity: "Normal" medical data is abundant; "Disease" data is rare and expensive to label.
  • The "Black Swan" Failure: Supervised models (Classifiers) cannot detect new or unknown diseases they haven't been trained on.
  • Lack of Explainability: Traditional AI gives a score ("98% Risk") but doesn't tell doctors where to look.

üí° The Solution: Unsupervised Learning

Instead of learning "What is Pneumonia?", we learn "What is Healthy?"

  • Step 1: Train purely on healthy anatomy (One-Class Classification).
  • Step 2: The model learns to reconstruct healthy lungs perfectly.
  • Step 3: When it sees a disease, it fails to reconstruct it.
  • Result: The "Reconstruction Error" highlights the tumor/infection automatically.

üß† Three Architectures Compared

Model Mechanism Pros Cons
VAE Probabilistic Compression (Gaussian Latent Space) Smooth, stable training. Reliable baselines. Blurry reconstructions.
GAN Adversarial Game (Generator vs Discriminator) Sharp, realistic details. High contrast. Unstable training (Mode Collapse).
ViT Self-Attention (16x16 Patches) Global context awareness. Modern approach. Data hungry; blocky artifacts on small datasets.
